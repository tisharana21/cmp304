A short story is a short, self-contained work of fiction that generally falls between 1,000 and 10,000 words. Because of this length constraint, short stories tend to be less complex than longer works—in certain ways.
In a short story, you can build a world, but not to the extent you can build a world in a (longer) novel. Similarly, you can have multiple fleshed-out characters, but you can’t give every character a full backstory and meaningful character arc like you can in a lengthier work. Generally, long, intricate plots with multiple subplots are better suited to novel-length works than a short story.
